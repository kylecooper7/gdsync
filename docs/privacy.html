gdsync

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 14, 2026

What gdsync does

gdsync is an open-source CLI tool that syncs Google Docs to a local file for editing. It connects to Google's APIs on your behalf to read and modify documents you have access to.

Data we access

When you authenticate with gdsync, the tool accesses:

  • Google Docs — to read and write document content for documents you explicitly open with gdsync
  • Google Drive — to list your accessible documents, read and create comments, and upload images
  • Basic profile info — your name and email address, used to identify your account in the CLI

Data we store

On your machine: OAuth tokens are stored locally at ~/.gdsync/credentials.json. Document content is stored in content.txt in your working directory. These files never leave your machine.

On our servers: During the setup and authentication process, the gdsync auth proxy (a Cloudflare Worker) temporarily handles OAuth token exchange. Tokens are stored in Cloudflare KV for up to 5 minutes during the sign-in flow, then automatically deleted. We do not retain any tokens, document content, or personal data after the auth flow completes.

Data we do NOT access

  • We do not read, store, or transmit the content of your Google Docs on any server
  • We do not access documents other than those you explicitly open with gdsync
  • We do not share any data with third parties
  • We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, or training

Your own GCP project

gdsync requires each user to create their own Google Cloud project. This means your API calls go through your own project — gdsync's infrastructure never has ongoing access to your documents. The auth proxy is only involved during the initial sign-in.

Open source

gdsync is open source. You can review the complete CLI source code at github.com/kylecooper7/gdsync to verify exactly what data is accessed and how.

Data deletion

To revoke gdsync's access, remove the app from your Google account at myaccount.google.com/permissions. To delete local data, remove ~/.gdsync/ and any working directories.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, open an issue at github.com/kylecooper7/gdsync/issues.
